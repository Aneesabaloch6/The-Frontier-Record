The Baloch Yakjehti Committee has condemned the decision to declare the Azad Jammu and Kashmir Joint Awami Action Committee (JAAC) a banned organization, describing the move as an attack on peaceful political expression and democratic participation.

According to the statement, banning a political movement does not remove the concerns, demands, or aspirations of the people it represents. Instead, it reflects a failure to address those concerns through political dialogue and democratic engagement.

The statement places the proscription of JAAC within what it describes as a broader pattern of restrictions on political movements in Pakistan. It references the earlier ban on the Pashtun Tahafuz Movement (PTM) and argues that such measures suppress political voices rather than addressing underlying grievances.

The committee calls on political parties, civil society organizations, students, workers, and democratic groups to oppose restrictions on freedom of expression, political participation, and peaceful mobilization. It argues that continued limitations on these rights could have wider implications for democratic freedoms across the country.

The statement concludes by asserting that demands for justice, dignity, and democratic rights cannot be eliminated through bans or coercive measures, and calls for the protection of peaceful political activity and public participation.
